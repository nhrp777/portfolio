Projects - Digital Electronics

Printed Circuit Board Design, DE

October 9 - 26, 2018

Project Objective: Design and create a printed circuit board (PCB) for a circuit of your choice.

For this project, I worked independently to create a light activated sensor with a 555 timer integrated circuit. When an external light source (such as a flashlight or a lamp) is shined on the light-dependent resistor (LDR), the light-emitting diode (LED) lights up. As soon as the light does not fall on the LDR, the LED no longer lights up. The circuit was first wired on a breadboard for testing purposes. A layout for the printed circuit board (PCB) was created using the program Fritzing. The PCB layout was then printed onto glossy paper and masked onto the copper PCB. A permanent marker was used to fill in portions of the PCB where the mask was unclear or broken. After being masked, the copper board was etched using ferric chloride solution. After drilling holes in the board and soldering on the components, the final solution was complete.

My final circuit PCB had only some of the desired functionality. I attempted to troubleshoot the circuit by checking all of the connections with a multimeter, and replacing and re-soldering components. When the batteries are placed in the battery holder, the LED instantly glowed. Although the LDR was detecting the changes in light, the circuit did not respond to any of these changes.

Although I was unsuccessful in reaching my final goal, this project was a valuable learning experience for me. It was my first exposure to PCB design techniques and electronics concepts that were completely unfamiliar to me before. I also gained firsthand experience with the painstaking process of troubleshooting an electronic circuit.

Combinational Logic Circuit Design: Displays, DE

January 15 - 31, 2019

Project Objective: Work as a team to display a message on a group of seven segment displays to simulate a LED display board.

In this project, I worked with three other team members to design, build, and test a circuit which displays a scrolling LED message. This message, "join DE", encourages high school students to join PLTW's Digital Electronics (DE) class and could be displayed at a school open house night. I was responsible for creating two of the eight seven-segment displays (SSDs) used in the circuit, as well as coordinating the group's daily tasks on an organized Gantt Chart. During the project timeline, I created truth tables, wrote logic expressions, electronically simulated, and breadboarded the two SSDs. Additionally, I calculated the efficiency of IC usage in the final group circuit, and contributed to the project documentation linked below.
