Projects - AP Computer Science A

Android Studio Mobile Application, CSA

December 8 - 15, 2017

Project Objective: Design, build, and test an Android mobile application using Android Studio.

In this project, we were asked to use Android Studio to develop a mobile application. I worked with 3 other students to develop the OrElse mobile application, which allows a student to store and prioritize upcoming homework assignments for each of their classes. The student will manually input each assignment, giving it a due date and priority multiplier, as well as inputting their current grade in the class. The assignments will then be displayed, and can be organized by priority multiplier, due date, or class grade. This application was designed and developed as a proof of concept -- all information was local to the device.

In my role, I designed and programmed the application's login screen, and documented the main part of the project. I also worked on designing the prototype in Proto.io, creating the Assignment List, and programming the add and remove assignment functions. This project helped me learn the fundamentals of app development in Android Studio; more specifically, user input, app navigation, and how to write JUnit test classes. We identified a problem in our community and learned how to logically isolate each component to address and solve it. I approached the problem through user-centric design, exploring how the user will interact with each component of our application.

Entrepreneurship Project, CSA

May 7 - 24, 2018

Project Objective: In a two week timeline, devise a unique and marketable product which benefits a target consumer and incorporates computer science concepts and skills.

I worked independently to prototype sporttracker, a mobile application designed to streamline the timecard hours verification process for high school student athletes enrolled in athletic PE. Under the current system, students physically bring their timecards to their coaches and the athletic director for signatures. Coaches and athletic directors find it difficult to monitor student progression and completion of hours. Also, physical timecards can get damaged or lost, and are not always accessible.

I first conducted background research about current athletic PE timecard systems in place at several local high schools. I then sketched out a concept for each screen on a notepad, created user stories, and prototyped the application using the online platform Fluid UI. Throughout the design process, I shared my concept with student athletes and coaches across various sports and recorded their feedback to make sporttracker as user-friendly as possible.

The final solution was designed with three tiers of users in mind: students, coaches, and athletic directors. After registering with the app and joining a sport, students can generate a virtual timecard and update their hours every week. The coaches and athletic directors will have a virtual roster of all of the students who registered with their school and sport, and will be able to electronically approve each student's hours for the week.

This project helped me gain further insight into the process of developing an app from start to finish. It also reinforced my knowledge of the engineering design process.
