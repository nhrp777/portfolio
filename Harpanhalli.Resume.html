Field Trips

As a part of the Dublin High School Engineering and Design Academy, I have attended multiple engineering field trips. In October 2016, I enjoyed attending EECS Day at UC Berkeley, hosted by the campus chapter of Eta Kappa Nu (HKN), an electrical engineering and computer science honor society. I experienced a day in the life of a EECS student at UC Berkeley, taking a department tour of the facilities and labs and listening to a lecture by EECS professor John DeNero. I loved meeting current UC Berkeley students, who guided us on the tour and shared their high school and college experiences.

In February 2017, I attended the RiskIQ Cybersecurity Converge Tour in San Francisco with 15 other female students. This event included an industry panel and networking lunch, as well as a cybersecurity capture the flag competition, where my partner and I won second place. It really interested me in the growing field of cybersecurity, a field I had never considered working in before.

In March 2018, I visited Google Headquarters in Mountain View, California. I had the opportunity to listen to a group of employees discuss how they worked to develop a Google product, Bulletin, and learned about how the engineering design process is applied to develop real-world applications. I also toured parts of the Google campus and some of the visitor attractions.

Guest Speakers

During my Principles of Engineering class and my time in the Engineering Academy junior mentorship program, I listened to fascinating talks from industry professionals including Ms. Danielle Demmon and Mr. Anthony Strawa. An electrical engineer at Lockheed Martin, Ms. Demmon vividly described electrical engineering and its many applications in her presentation. It was particularly interesting to hear how electrical engineering is connected to computer science and biomedical engineering, two fields I am interested in pursuing in college. Mr. Strawa, the director of the New Opportunities office at the NASA Ames Research Center, discussed innovative technologies and interdisciplinary scientific discoveries. I learned a lot about aerospace engineering from Mr. Strawa's presentation, which also included some interesting details about past NASA projects concerned with extraterrestrial life.

Junior Year Mentorship Program

During the 2017-2018 school year, I had the unique opportunity of participating in a one-year mentorship program within the Dublin High School Engineering and Design Academy. My mentor, Ms. Erin Chia, has a PhD in biomedical engineering and works at Bio-Rad Laboratories. Although we had different fields of interest, I enjoyed discussing my academic progress and achievements with her. We spent time exploring different colleges and careers, and working on resume building and goal setting. I also had the chance to network with other program mentors, and thus learned about different engineering disciplines. This program was a very meaningful experience and I definitely valued being a part of it.
