Work Experience:

As a part of the Dublin High School Engineering and Design Academy, I have attended multiple engineering field trips. In October 2016, I enjoyed attending EECS Day at UC Berkeley, hosted by the campus chapter of Eta Kappa Nu (HKN), an electrical engineering and computer science honor society. I experienced a day in the life of a EECS student at UC Berkeley, taking a department tour of the facilities and labs and listening to a lecture by EECS professor John DeNero. I loved meeting current UC Berkeley students, who guided us on the tour and shared their high school and college experiences. In February 2017, I attended the RiskIQ Cybersecurity Converge Tour in San Francisco with 15 other female students. This event included an industry panel and networking lunch, as well as a cybersecurity capture the flag competition, where my partner and I won second place. It really interested me in the growing field of cybersecurity, a field I had never considered working in before. This year, I visited Google Headquarters in Mountain View, California. I had the opportunity to listen to a group of employees discuss how they worked to develop a Google product, Bulletin, and learned about how the engineering design process is applied to develop real-world applications. I also toured parts of the Google campus and some of the visitor attractions.

During my Principles of Engineering class and my time in the Engineering Academy junior mentorship program, I heard talks by guest speakers including Ms. Danielle Demmon from Lockheed Martin and Ms. Erin Chia from Bio-Rad Laboratories. In her presentation, Ms. Demmon described electrical engineering and its many applications; it was particularly interesting to hear how it connected to computer science and biomedical technologies, two fields I am interested in pursuing. Ms. Chia spoke about her background in biomedical and chemical engineering, and her work with polymers, which fascinated me due to my growing passion for biology and chemistry.

This year, I had the unique opportunity of participating in a one-year mentorship program within the Dublin High School Engineering and Design Academy. My mentor, Ms. Erin Chia, has a PhD in biomedical engineering and works at Bio-Rad Laboratories. Although we have slightly different fields of interest, I enjoyed discussing my academic progress with her. We also spent time with college and career exploration, resume building, and goal setting. This program was a very meaningful experience and I definitely learned a lot through my exposure to different engineering disciplines.
